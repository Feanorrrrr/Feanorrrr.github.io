About Me

I am an Electrical Engineering student who enjoys learning through hands-on projects in both hardware and software. My interests include embedded systems, digital design, engineering visualization, and practical software development.

I like building things that connect technical theory with real implementation, whether that means working on FPGA systems, embedded devices, or software tools for engineering applications.

Background

My interest in electrical engineering started early. As a child, I was curious about how devices worked and wanted to understand what was happening inside them. One of my earliest memories is trying to figure out why a radio at my grandmother’s house was making a strange sizzling sound. I was too young to fix it at the time, but that moment stayed with me and became one of the reasons I became interested in engineering.

As I grew older, that curiosity developed in different ways. I became interested in programming, circuit design, and building systems from scratch. I also spent time experimenting with redstone in Minecraft, where I enjoyed creating logic-based mechanisms and simple computational structures. Over time, I realized that what I enjoyed most was not just using technology, but understanding it and creating with it.

Today, I continue to build on that interest through projects in embedded systems, FPGA design, visualization, and engineering software. I am especially interested in work that connects hardware and software in a practical way, and I hope to keep developing tools and systems that are both technically meaningful and useful in real applications.
